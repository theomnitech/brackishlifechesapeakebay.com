Protecting the Heart of the Mid-Atlantic: The Chesapeake Bay

Why the Chesapeake Bay Matters

The Chesapeake Bay is the largest estuary in the United States, stretching over 200 miles and supporting more than 3,600 species of plants and animals. It is a vital ecosystem that balances fresh and saltwater influences, creating brackish environments that sustain life, culture, and commerce.

Its marshes and tributaries are not only home to blue crabs, oysters, and waterfowl, but also to generations of families who live, work, and find peace along its shores. However, pollution, overharvesting, and development threaten its delicate balance.
